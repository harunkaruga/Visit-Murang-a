Overview

Murang’a County is located in central Kenya and is known for its green hills, agricultural richness, and friendly residents. Historically referred to as Fort Hall, Murang’a is a peaceful destination full of charm and local flavor.

Top Attractions

Whether you're a nature lover, a cultural enthusiast, or just looking to relax, Murang’a has something to offer. Popular spots include:

  • Aberdare Ranges – Ideal for hiking and wildlife watching
  • Ndakaini Dam – A scenic spot perfect for photography and leisure
  • Kigumo Tea Plantations – Enjoy a guided tour and taste fresh local tea

Culture & People

The people of Murang’a, primarily from the Kikuyu community, are known for their strong work ethic and deep-rooted traditions. Local markets are vibrant and full of fresh produce, handmade crafts, and storytelling.

Visit Murang’a

Interested in experiencing Murang’a? Plan your trip during the dry season (June–October) and get ready for an unforgettable journey through one of Kenya’s most underrated counties.
